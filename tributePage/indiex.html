Here's a time line of Indian freedom fighter Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose:

  • Jan 23, 1897 ‐ Born in Cuttack, Orissa, India
  • Jan 1, 1916 ‐ Expelled from Presidency College in Calcutta for nationalistic activities.
  • Jan 1, 1919 ‐ He graduated from Scottish Churches College.
  • Jan 1, 1920 ‐ He passed the civil service exam after studying at the University of Cambridge.
  • Apr 1, 1921 ‐ Resigned from the Indian Civil Service.
  • Dec 1, 1921 ‐ Sent to jail by the British due to his activities with the Bengal Congress Volunteers.
  • Jan 1, 1927 ‐ Upon being released from prison he found the Bengal Congress in disarray.
  • Jan 1, 1936 ‐ Returned from Europe — Upon arriving he was taken into custody and released a year later.
  • Jan 1, 1938 ‐ Elected president of the Indian National Congress and formed a national planning committee, which formulated a policy of broad industrialization.
  • Jan 1, 1939 ‐ Resigned from Presidency — He felt like he needed to resign because of a lack of support from Gandhi.
  • Jul 1, 1940 ‐ Incarcerated Yet Again — Was Incarcerated due to his creation of the Foward Bloc. His refusal to remain in prison at this critical period of India’s history was expressed in a determination to fast to death, which frightened the British government into releasing him.
  • Jan 26, 1941 ‐Escaped from Prison — though closely watched, he escaped from his Calcutta residence in disguise and, traveling via Kabul and Moscow, eventually reached Germany in April.
  • Jan 24, 1942 ‐ He and other Indians who had gathered in Berlin made regular broadcasts from the German-sponsored Azad Hind Radio beginning in January 1942, speaking in English, Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Gujarati, and Pashto.
  • May 1, 1943 ‐ A little more than a year after the Japanese invasion of Southeast Asia, Bose left Germany, traveling by German and Japanese submarines and by plane, and arrived in May 1943 in Tokyo.
  • Jun 4, 1943 ‐ A little more than a year after the Japanese invasion of Southeast Asia, Bose left Germany, traveling by German and Japanese submarines and by plane, and arrived in May 1943 in Tokyo.
  • Mar 18, 1944 ‐ His Army Reached Indian Soil — In a stubborn battle, the mixed Indian and Japanese forces, lacking Japanese air support, were defeated and forced to retreat; the Indian National Army nevertheless for some time succeeded in maintaining its identity as a liberation army, based in Burma and then Indochina.
  • Aug 18, 1945 ‐ A few days after Japan’s announced surrender in August 1945, Bose, fleeing Southeast Asia, reportedly died in a Japanese hospital in Taiwan as a result of burn injuries from a plane crash.
